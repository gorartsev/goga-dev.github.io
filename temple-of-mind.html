Temple of the Mind

A first-person puzzle adventure built in Unity (URP). The player enters an ancient temple hub and unlocks five biomes by solving environmental, architecture-driven puzzles. My main focus: spatial clarity, visual guidance, and clean greybox flow.

Level Goals

• Build a readable approach to the main temple hub.
• Establish silhouette, rhythm, and first-playable pacing.
• Guide the player with composition instead of UI.
• Prepare a solid greybox foundation for future biomes.

Player Flow

The core loop: explore → read environment → solve mechanism → unlock gate. The hub visually reflects progress through lighting lines and activated doors.

Blocking – First Location

Early greybox for the entrance path: focus on scale, sightlines and the reveal of the hub. No details yet — only pure layout.

Puzzle Logic

• All clues are embedded into architecture (shapes, alignment, symbols). • No UI hints — the player reads space visually. • Puzzles give strong feedback: movement of stone, light shifts, sound cues. • Each biome introduces a new mechanic variation.

Technical Notes

Unity URP · First-person controller · Baked + mixed lighting Raycast interaction · Modular kits · Clean greybox workflow
