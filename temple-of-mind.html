Temple of the Mind

Temple of the Mind is a first-person puzzle adventure built in Unity. The player explores an ancient temple hub and opens ritual gates to 3 biomes, each with its own architecture and environmental puzzles. My focus is on spatial readability, environmental storytelling and puzzle design without UI hints.

Role: Level Design, Worldbuilding, Lighting Engine: Unity · URP · First-person Target: PC · Puzzle Adventure

Level 1 environment showcase — the first chamber leading toward the central temple hub. Focus: composition, lighting, mood, stone material language.

Worldbuilding & Fantasy

The temple is a forgotten ritual complex in the desert. At its centre lies the "Riddle of Humanity" – an artefact sealed behind a multi-stage ritual. To reach it, the player must activate the hub, travel through five biomes and return with fragments of a single key.

The tone is calm and mystical: stone, sand and distant echoes instead of loud music. Architecture carries most of the story – walls, pillars and floors hide mechanisms and clues rather than relying on dialogue or UI.

Structure, Flow & Puzzles

The game is built around a central hub and two biomes: Forest and Desert, followed by the final Tomb. Each biome adds a small twist to the same environmental puzzle language and still serves one goal: assemble the key and unlock the heart of the temple.

Gameplay loop:
• read a clue in the hub → open a gate
• explore a biome and solve 2–3 architecture-driven puzzles
• earn a key fragment and return to the hub
• activate a slot and light a new "energy channel" in the architecture
• when all channels are active, the final tomb opens

Puzzle language:
• clues are embedded in walls, floors and props – no floating markers
• the goal of each puzzle is readable in a few seconds
• strong feedback: stone movement, light shifts, rumbling audio
• later puzzles reuse previous knowledge instead of adding new rules
