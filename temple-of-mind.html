Temple of the Mind

Temple of the Mind is a first-person puzzle adventure built in Unity (URP). The player explores an ancient temple-hub and opens magical gates to five distinct biomes — each with its own architecture, atmosphere and environmental puzzles inspired by The Room, The Witness and Uncharted. All clues are embedded directly into the environment: architecture, reliefs, rock carvings and props.

Role: Level Design, Worldbuilding, Lighting Engine: Unity · URP · First-person Target: PC · Puzzle Adventure

Worldbuilding & Fantasy

Temple of the Mind is presented as a forgotten sacred complex hidden in the desert. The player is an explorer who discovers a mosaic about the “Riddle of Humanity” — an artefact sealed in the heart of the temple. To reach it, they must unlock the first ritual gate, pass through five biomes and combine the fragments of a single key.

Tone & atmosphere:
• ancient, mystical, slightly unsettling but calm
• “sounding silence”: sand, stone, distant echoes instead of loud music
• architecture itself is a puzzle – walls, pillars and floors hide mechanisms
• every space looks like it has a secret, even before you touch anything

The core fantasy is to feel like an Indiana Jones–style researcher standing on the threshold of a lost civilisation, where architecture, light and materials are the main storytellers, not dialogue or UI.

Structure, Biomes & Flow

Structurally the game is built around a central temple hub and five biomes accessed through ritual gates: Forest, Pyramid, Ruined Temple, Coastal Biome and Ice Cave – followed by the final Tomb. Each biome introduces its own materials, puzzle formats and mood, while feeding back into the same overarching goal: assemble the key and open the central chamber.

High-level flow:
• solve a hub puzzle → open a gate → enter a biome
• complete 3 mid-sized puzzles (or 1 large + 1–2 supporting)
• earn a fragment of the key (totem / symbol / crystal – TBD)
• return through the portal → activate a slot in the hub
• once all fragments are placed, the final tomb opens

Each completed biome also activates a visible “energy channel” in the temple — a glowing line or conduit that helps players read progression and understand which gate unlocks next, similar to cable language in The Witness.

Environmental Puzzles

The entire experience is built around environmental puzzles and embedded clues. There are no floating markers or pop-up hints: the player is expected to observe, compare and connect information entirely through space, composition and detail.

Puzzle ingredients:
• totems and ritual statues
• sliding stone slabs and rotating elements
• hidden niches and secret compartments in walls
• symbol matching across architecture and props
• layered architectural mechanisms that shift the whole space

Design philosophy:
• understand the goal in ~5 seconds (what needs to change)
• explore the surroundings to find clues embedded in the environment
• apply the clue, experiment, resolve
• get strong feedback: stone movement, light changes, rumbling audio

Key Moments & Emotional Beats

The level structure is built around a chain of memorable beats that combine composition, pacing and sound. The aim is to move from curiosity and light unease to a sense of clarity and completion.

Examples of moments I’m designing for:
• first approach to the temple hub with a strong layered vista and sealed gates

• reading the main mosaic that hints at the five biomes and the final artefact

• entering each biome through a narrow stone corridor into a radically different world

• returning to the hub and seeing newly lit “energy channels” connecting gates

• final descent into the tomb where all previous motifs converge into one space

Technical Notes (Unity)

Engine & rendering:
• Unity (URP), PC target
• first-person controller with slow, deliberate movement
• baked + mixed lighting for a stable, atmospheric look
• fog and simple volumetric effects to layer depth in large halls
• modular meshes for temple structures and biome-specific kits

Interaction:
• raycast-based interaction for looking at and using objects
• click / hold to rotate and drag movable elements
• subtle outline / glow shader for interactive pieces (diegetic, not “UI yellow”)
• minimal on-screen interface – focus on world readability

Workflow & next steps:
• hub greybox → first biome → iteration on puzzle readability
• add clear visual “progress channels” from hub to each gate
• refine pacing between puzzles and calm traversal spaces
• build a short recorded walkthrough of the hub and one biome to demonstrate the full loop
