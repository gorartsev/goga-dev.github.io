Temple of Mind

Atmospheric exploration prototype built in Unity (URP) — a surreal bioluminescent temple-city that exists somewhere between architecture, consciousness and memory. This is my main personal project focused on mood-driven traversal, readability and environmental storytelling for a PC/console-style experience.

Worldbuilding & Concept

The core idea behind Temple of Mind is a sacred structure assembled from fragments of consciousness instead of traditional stone and brick. Each hall and corridor reflects a specific mental state: calm, doubt, conflict, clarity. Light behaves like thought, space folds in subtle ways, and organic stone quietly reacts to the player’s presence.

Visual language:
• monolithic silhouettes with minimal readable detail
• repeating architectural motifs to build rhythm and memory
• soft bioluminescent accents instead of aggressive UI markers
• depth-based readability where silhouettes come first, micro-detail second

The goal is to create an environment that can be explored slowly and deliberately, where the player understands the path forward through composition, contrast and spatial hierarchy rather than waypoints.

Level Design & Flow

The current version of the project is focused on one main route that passes through several “mental zones”. Each zone changes the pace, lighting and density of detail to create a different emotional beat. The layout is built through iterative blockouts in Unity, starting from simple primitives and evolving into modular architecture.

Design pillars:
• Readability: clear silhouettes, foreground/background separation, use of contrast and height to show direction.
• Traversal: ramps, staircases and platforms that feel heavy and architectural, not “gamey”, with deliberate pauses for vistas.
• Mood: fog, color grading and local light sources define how “safe” or “hostile” a space feels, without explicit enemies.
• Discovery: side paths and small pockets of space reward curiosity with new views, hidden shrines or visual storytelling props.

Key Moments & Beats

The level is structured around a few memorable moments: first contact with the temple, descent into the inner halls, and a calmer final area that feels like a mental “release”. Each beat combines camera framing, lighting and sound to reinforce the feeling of moving deeper into someone’s mind.

Examples of moments I’m designing for:
• arrival at the main staircase with a strong, layered vista

• narrow, almost claustrophobic corridor that opens into a vast chamber

• elevated balconies overlooking areas the player has already visited, reinforcing spatial memory

• quiet “sanctuary” zones where music and light are intentionally softer

Technical Notes (Unity)

Engine & Rendering:
• Unity (URP) with baked + mixed lighting setup
• Atmospheric fog, simple volumetric effects for depth
• Modular meshes for temple structures (pillars, walls, arches, stairs)

Workflow:
• greybox → playtest → refine silhouettes → detail pass
• focus on keeping navigation colliders clean and readable
• camera tuning for slow, exploration-focused movement

Next steps:
• polish traversal rhythm and pacing between “hub” and sub-areas
• add more interactive environmental elements (small rituals, shrines)
• build a short recorded walkthrough to show the intended experience
