Temple of the Mind

Temple of the Mind is a first-person puzzle adventure built in Unity. The player wakes up in an old cursed temple, solves a set of ritual puzzles to escape and then steps into a sunny, slightly stylised forest clearing – the Heart of the Forest. In the centre stands an ancient stump-altar with three slots for artefacts. From this hub the player chooses between two seasonal forest paths – an autumn grove and a frozen winter woods – to complete the ritual and unlock the way to a distant castle on the hill.

Role: Level Design, Worldbuilding, Lighting Engine: Unity · URP · First-person Target: PC · Puzzle Adventure

Level 1 environment showcase — the intro sequence leading from the cursed temple toward the first glimpse of the outside world. Focus: composition, lighting, mood, stone material language.

Worldbuilding & Fantasy

The world combines light horror with fairytale fantasy. The game opens in an old ritual temple where a failed ceremony has trapped the protagonist. To escape, the player must reactivate parts of the ritual, find a way to open the main gate and step out of the oppressive stone corridors.

Outside, the tone shifts into a calm, magical forest. On a bright clearing the player finds the Heart of the Forest – a massive stump with a carved, sleeping face and three slots for artefacts. One artefact is found here, two are hidden deeper in the woods: in an autumn forest and a cold, snowy forest. When all three artefacts are returned to the stump, ancient gates open the path toward a castle visible high on a distant hill.

Structure, Flow & Puzzles

The structure is a short, focused sequence of spaces: the cursed temple intro, the forest hub clearing with the stump-altar, two seasonal forest branches and a planned follow-up chapter set in the castle. All locations reuse the same ritual language: find artefacts, read the environment, complete the altar to move forward.

Gameplay loop:
• wake up in the cursed temple and solve a ritual puzzle to reach the outside
• step onto the sunny forest clearing and discover the stump-altar + first artefact
• choose a direction: left into the autumn grove, right into the snowy winter woods
• in each branch, solve 1–2 environmental puzzles to earn an artefact
• return to the stump and place all three artefacts to open the gate toward the castle

Puzzle language:
• clues are carved or painted on walls, floors, tree trunks and the stump itself – no floating markers
• the goal of each puzzle is readable in a few seconds
• strong feedback: stone movement, light shifts, rumbling audio, forest ambience
• later puzzles reuse previously learned patterns instead of adding new rules

Level Plans

Blocking – Intro: Cursed Temple

This is the first playable area inside the temple. In greybox I focus on player height, tight sightlines, silhouettes of key doors and a clear but slightly winding path toward the escape point. The goal is to create a feeling of tension and mystery while still keeping navigation readable.

Blocking – Forest Hub & Seasonal Paths

The second major location takes the player into the Heart of the Forest and the first iteration of the surrounding woods. The layout creates an illusion of boundless forest while gently steering the player back toward the hub and the stump-altar. The player can choose a path that eventually loops back without feeling like they hit a wall.

In blocking I focus on rhythm: clusters of trees, controlled sightlines toward the stump, fog pockets and subtle framing of future points of interest. This greybox validates how the hub, the “left” and the “right” paths (autumn and winter forest branches) connect and how the player naturally returns to the clearing after each puzzle.

Final Environment Shots — Heart of the Forest

After validating the layout and navigation in greybox, the forest moves into its visual pass. The focus here is on atmosphere, depth and readability while keeping the biome calm, magical and slightly disorienting – the player should feel safe but not fully in control.

These shots show early lighting and fog layers, vegetation density tests and first composition passes for guiding the player between the clearing, side paths and key puzzle spots. The stump-altar and the idea of seasonal contrast (warmer vs. colder areas) drive the final art direction for this biome.
