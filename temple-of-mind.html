01

Level Plan: Ancient Temple

The temple layout follows a three-zone structure: Entrance Corridor (Zone C), Main Temple Hall (Zone B), and Puzzle Room (Zone A). Each zone serves a specific purpose in the player's learning journey before they face the central puzzle monument.

Top-down layout with player flow
Design Challenge
Teaching Without UI
How do you teach a symbol-matching puzzle without floating markers or tutorial pop-ups? The grimoire placement before the puzzle panel ensures players read the clue before attempting the solution.
Design Challenge
Exploration as Reward
Four symbols scattered across three zones encourage players to explore the full space. Each symbol location rewards attention to environmental detail: at guardian feet, between columns, in corners.
Design Challenge
Readable Sightlines
From the entrance corridor, players can glimpse the main hall through a narrow opening, creating anticipation while framing the destination. The locked gate remains visible as a constant goal.
02

Player Flow

The level guides players through a carefully paced sequence, from disorientation to discovery to mastery.

  1. Spawn in the corridor. Torches draw the eye forward. The narrow space creates tension and establishes scale.
  2. Enter the Main Temple Hall. Columns frame the space. Bodies on the floor suggest a failed ritual. The glowing artifact above hints at what went wrong.
  3. Climb the staircase. Elevation change signals transition to the puzzle zone. The locked gate becomes visible, and the goal is now clear.
  4. Find the Grimoire. Positioned before the puzzle panel so players read the clue first. The hint shows which four symbols to find.
  5. Search for symbols. Scattered through all three zones: at the guardian's feet (Zone A), between columns (Zone B), and in corners. Exploration rewarded.
  6. Solve the puzzle. Enter symbols in the correct order. The gate opens. Strong audio and visual feedback confirms success.

Location 02

The Enchanted Forest

A magical clearing with four seasons meeting at the center. Collect three relics to unlock the gate to the castle.

01

Level Plan: The Enchanted Forest

The forest uses a hub-and-spoke layout with the Central Clearing as the main anchor point. Players exit the temple, find the first relic on the path, then explore two puzzle areas (Stone Circle and Ancient Tree) to collect the remaining relics. All paths return to the hub where the altar awaits.

Hub-based layout with puzzle loops
Structure
Hub as Anchor
The Central Clearing serves as a constant reference point. No matter which puzzle the player pursues, all paths lead back to the altar. This prevents disorientation in an open environment.
Progression
Non-Linear Choice
After placing the first relic, players choose between Puzzle 1 (Stone Circle) or Puzzle 2 (Ancient Tree). Both are visible from the hub, giving players agency while maintaining clear goals.
Boundaries
Forest as Wall
Dense tree lines form natural boundaries. Players understand they cannot pass through the forest, so they stay on the paths. No invisible walls needed.
02

Player Flow

The forest level introduces freedom after the linear temple. Players learn the loop: explore, solve, return, place.

  1. Exit the Temple. The doors close behind the player. A dirt path leads forward through dense trees. The first relic glows on the ground ahead.
  2. Find Relic 1. Placed directly on the main path so every player picks it up naturally. This teaches the core mechanic: collect and carry.
  3. Reach the Central Clearing. The hub opens up with the altar in the center. Three slots visible. The player places Relic 1 and sees two more are needed.
  4. Choose a direction. Two paths lead to two puzzle areas. Stone Circle to the left, Ancient Tree to the right. Both visible from the clearing.
  5. Solve Puzzle 1 or Puzzle 2. Each puzzle has its own logic and rewards a relic. After solving, the player returns to the hub and places the relic.
  6. Complete the ritual. All three relics placed. The gate to the castle opens. Strong visual and audio feedback marks the victory moment.
03

Blockout Phase

The forest greybox validates scale, path clarity, and landmark visibility. Simple cylinder trees establish the boundary density. The altar and puzzle locations are tested for sightlines from every approach angle.

04

Final Environment

The forest features four seasons meeting at the center: spring greens, autumn golds, winter whites, and the magical lake. Each puzzle area has a distinct seasonal mood. Giant mushrooms, flowering meadows, and the Ancient Tree create memorable landmarks that players use for navigation.
